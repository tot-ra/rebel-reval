# Tourist Landmarks: Modern Estonia vs. 1343 Reval

This document catalogs notable tourist landmarks in modern Tallinn and Estonia,
mapping each to its **1343 status** during the events of *Reval Rebel*.
Landmarks that did not exist in 1343 are listed only in the exclusion appendix.

## Overview

Each landmark includes:
- **Modern Status**: Current state as a tourist destination
- **1343 Status**: Historical context during the game's setting
- **Lore Tie-in**: Connection to in-game factions, characters, and map locations

**Counts:** 108 Tallinn landmarks grouped by district; 100 elsewhere in Estonia.

For historical context, factions, and characters see [`docs/CANON.md`](CANON.md).
For map authoring boundaries see [`docs/HISTORICAL_AUDIT.md`](HISTORICAL_AUDIT.md).

# Part I: Tallinn (Reval) by District

## Toompea (Upper Town)

### 1. Toompea Castle (Toompea loss)
* **Modern Status:** Seat of the Estonian Parliament with a pink Baroque palace and Tall Hermann tower.
* **1343 Status:** A stark Danish stone castle on the hilltop; Tall Hermann tower is not built yet.
* **Lore Tie-in:** Ultimate target of the St. George's Night Uprising; Danish viceroy and Order envoys hold court here.

### 2. St. Mary's Cathedral (Toomkirik)
* **Modern Status:** Oldest church in Tallinn, lined with Baltic German noble coats of arms.
* **1343 Status:** Spiritual center of Toompea serving Danish and German elite since the 13th century.
* **Lore Tie-in:** Ellen Luik aids families the Cathedral's formal charity overlooks.

### 3. Danish King's Garden (Taani Kuninga aed)
* **Modern Status:** Scenic viewpoint tied to the 1219 Dannebrog legend.
* **1343 Status:** Private elevated garden for Danish rulers, steeped in conquest mythology.
* **Lore Tie-in:** Lower-town rebels look up at the garden as a symbol of foreign rule.

### 4. Toompea viewing platforms (Patkuli & Kohtuotsa)
* **Modern Status:** Popular photo terraces overlooking the lower town and harbor.
* **1343 Status:** Unpaved ramparts and patrol walks on the castle's outer edge.
* **Lore Tie-in:** Rebel scouts memorize sightlines from these heights before the siege.

### 5. Long Leg Gate (Pikk jalg värav)
* **Modern Status:** Steep fortified passage linking upper and lower town.
* **1343 Status:** Heavily guarded stone gate controlling movement between All-linn and Toompea.
* **Lore Tie-in:** Kaja times courier runs when the watch changes at dusk.

### 6. Short Leg Gate (Lühike jalg värav)
* **Modern Status:** Narrow stairway between Toompea and the Dominican quarter.
* **1343 Status:** A tight controlled stair under castle surveillance.
* **Lore Tie-in:** Mart uses the passage when monastery contacts want no Viru Gate record.

### 7. Toompea eastern ramparts
* **Modern Status:** Restored wall walks with cannon displays.
* **1343 Status:** Curtain walls and timber hoardings facing the lower town.
* **Lore Tie-in:** Danish archers train here during the April siege alarms.

### 8. Toompea western slope
* **Modern Status:** Tree-lined paths toward the former Bishop's Garden.
* **1343 Status:** Open slope with orchards and service paths outside the main keep.
* **Lore Tie-in:** Archbishop's household staff haul supplies along the western track.

### 9. Danish royal chapel site
* **Modern Status:** Later absorbed into castle fabric; no separate modern chapel.
* **1343 Status:** Small castle chapel serving the garrison and visiting envoys.
* **Lore Tie-in:** Order clerics negotiate safe passage for hostages near this chapel.

### 10. Toompea well courtyard
* **Modern Status:** Paved service yard within the castle complex.
* **1343 Status:** Castle well and storehouses for siege provisions.
* **Lore Tie-in:** Rumors of hidden grain here fuel lower-town bread riots.

### 11. Toompea guard barracks

# Appendix: Excluded Modern Landmarks (not present in 1343)

These popular tourist sites are **omitted** from the main catalog because
their current buildings or uses did not exist in 1343. Where a guild or
institution existed in simpler form, see the district entries above.

- **Alexander Nevsky Cathedral** - Built 1900 on Toompea. **1343:** Orthodox cathedral absent; castle courtyard occupies the site.

- **Kiek in de Kök artillery tower** - 15th-century cannon tower museum. **1343:** Tower not built; standard curtain wall only.

- **Fat Margaret (Paks Margareeta)** - 16th-century harbor artillery tower. **1343:** Harbor barbican not yet constructed.

- **Kadriorg Palace and Park** - Baroque palace of Peter the Great. **1343:** Forest and streams; no palace or formal park.

- **Pirita St. Bridget's Convent** - Ruins of a 1407 convent. **1343:** Founded 1407; site is coastal forest in 1343.

- **Great Guild Hall (monumental building)** - Current hall from 1407-1410. **1343:** Guild functions exist in simpler rooms; monumental hall not built.

- **House of the Blackheads (ornate house)** - Façade from 1597. **1343:** Brotherhood exists; grand house not yet built.

- **Tallinn TV Tower** - 20th-century broadcast tower. **1343:** No structure; open landscape.

- **Linnahall** - 1970s concert hall on the harbor. **1343:** Rocky foreshore and working quays only.

- **Song Festival Grounds (Lauluväljak)** - Modern amphitheatre. **1343:** Open fields outside the wall.
