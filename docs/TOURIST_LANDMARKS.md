# Tourist Landmarks: Modern Tallinn vs. 1343 Reval

This document maps 10 popular modern tourist landmarks in Tallinn to their historical status and function in the year 1343, during the events of *Reval Rebel*. This helps ground the game's locations in reality while explaining architectural differences to modern visitors.

## 1. Tallinn Town Hall (Raekoda)
* **Modern Status:** The iconic Gothic town hall dominating the main square, built in 1402–1404.
* **1343 Status:** A smaller, simpler stone and wooden building exists on the site (first mentioned in 1322). It serves as the administrative heart of the lower town (*All-linn*).
* **Lore Tie-in:** Hanseatic merchants like Jürgen Witte congregate here to negotiate taxes and manipulate the city watch. Kalev occasionally receives mundane commissions here to repair city property.

## 2. Toompea Castle (Toompea loss)
* **Modern Status:** The seat of the Estonian Parliament, featuring a pink Baroque palace and the Tall Hermann tower.
* **1343 Status:** A stark, heavily fortified stone castle controlled by the Danish viceroy. The Tall Hermann tower does not exist yet. It is the symbol of foreign oppression.
* **Lore Tie-in:** The ultimate target of the St. George's Night Uprising. Factions scheme to breach its walls, and Kalev's weapons might eventually find their way to its gates.

## 3. St. Olaf's Church (Oleviste kirik)
* **Modern Status:** Famous for its towering spire that once made it one of the tallest buildings in the world (rebuilt after fires).
* **1343 Status:** An active, heavily utilized 13th-century church, though structurally less imposing than its later 15th-century iteration.
* **Lore Tie-in:** Mart, Kalev's apprentice, sometimes meets his contacts in the bustling, less strictly patrolled courtyard of Oleviste to exchange whispers about the rural rebellion.

## 4. St. Nicholas Church (Niguliste kirik)
* **Modern Status:** An art museum and concert hall known for the *Danse Macabre* painting.
* **1343 Status:** The wealthy parish church of the German Hanseatic merchants, practically a fortress of its own.
* **Lore Tie-in:** Jürgen Witte's faction uses the church's influence and secure crypts to store sensitive contracts and perhaps hoarded goods. It stands as a monument to the economic divide Kalev faces daily.

## 5. St. Mary's Cathedral (Toomkirik / Dome Church)
* **Modern Status:** The oldest church in Tallinn, filled with the coats of arms of Baltic German nobility.
* **1343 Status:** The spiritual center of Toompea, serving the Danish and German elite.
* **Lore Tie-in:** A point of tension where the Catholic clergy preach obedience to the ruling class. Ellen Luik, the midwife, secretly aids those whom the Cathedral's charity overlooks, blending old faith with new.

## 6. Dominican Monastery (St. Catherine's Monastery)
* **Modern Status:** Atmospheric ruins and the picturesque St. Catherine's Passage (*Katariina käik*) filled with artisan workshops.
* **1343 Status:** A powerful, active monastery (founded 1246) playing a major role in education and brewing.
* **Lore Tie-in:** Aita, Kalev's sister, relies on the monks' herbal knowledge and occasionally trades her ale for their exotic medicinal ingredients. 

## 7. The City Wall & Viru Gate (Linnamüür & Viru värav)
* **Modern Status:** Well-preserved medieval walls and the romanticized twin towers of Viru Gate.
* **1343 Status:** The Margaret Wall (ordered 1265) exists but is shorter and has fewer towers. Viru Gate is functional but lacks the elaborate 15th-century foregates.
* **Lore Tie-in:** Captain Henning of the Viru Watch patrols this exact sector. Kaja, the bilingual courier, frequently smuggles messages and contraband through the Viru Gate under Henning's nose.

## 8. Alexander Nevsky Cathedral
* **Modern Status:** A grand, highly visible Orthodox cathedral on Toompea, built in 1900.
* **1343 Status:** Does not exist. The site is a courtyard/garden within the Toompea castle complex.
* **Lore Tie-in:** Since the cathedral is absent, the open space serves as a mustering ground for Danish guards and Livonian Order envoys, heavily guarded and completely inaccessible to commoners like Kalev.

## 9. Kiek in de Kök (Artillery Tower)
* **Modern Status:** A massive 15th-century cannon tower, now a museum.
* **1343 Status:** Does not exist. The walls in this section are standard curtain walls.
* **Lore Tie-in:** The vulnerability of the defenses in this area (before the tower's construction) is noted by rebel scouts. Kaja passes this architectural intelligence to the \"Four Kings.\"

## 10. Danish King's Garden (Taani Kuninga aed)
* **Modern Status:** A scenic viewpoint on Toompea, legend says the Danish flag (Dannebrog) fell from the sky here in 1219.
* **1343 Status:** A private, elevated garden for the Danish rulers, steeped in the mythology of their conquest.
* **Lore Tie-in:** A symbol of the initial colonization. Rebel sympathizers in the lower town look up at the garden with disdain. It serves as an atmospheric backdrop for secret meetings among the elite.
