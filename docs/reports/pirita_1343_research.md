# Pirita in 1343 - research and map translation

Recorded: 2026-07-22  
Scope: inactive `viru_gate_foreland` prototype, retained as a stable technical ID  
Confidence: conservative rural river-crossing reconstruction, not a measured 1343 plan

## Decision

- Present the location to players as **Pirita** and rebuild it around the Pirita River. Keep the existing `viru_gate_foreland` map, scene, file and transition IDs because they are already referenced by district transitions and developer saves.
- Enlarge the authored map from `112 x 80` to `168 x 120` cells, exactly 1.5 times in both dimensions.
- Run the Pirita River from south to north through the full map. Use a simple timber bridge to carry the Reval-Iru/Harju road across it. The river valley is attested as a long-lived movement corridor, but the exact 1343 bridge, road line and farm boundaries are not established, so the crossing is a reversible gameplay reconstruction.
- Make Pirita visibly different from the walled town through open river meadow, reed and alder banks, field/fallow strips, hay meadow, fenced farmyards, thatched timber buildings, sheep and horses. Animal placement is deliberately sparse and represents plausible working/tethered livestock, not a proven site-specific herd.
- Do **not** show St Bridget's church, convent walls, construction works or a predictive chapel. The founding initiative belongs to about 1400, Bridgettine advisers arrived in 1407, construction began after the 1417 quarry permit, and the church was consecrated in 1436. In 1343 the later convent plot is represented only by an open, unnamed river meadow.
- Do not use `history/AVE2013_09_Malvejt_Kose.pdf` as Pirita-Kose evidence. It studies Kose parish church much farther upstream and does not establish a church in the modern Tallinn subdistrict of Kose.

## Evidence and limits

1. Eesti Entsüklopeedia describes the 105 km Pirita River as flowing into Tallinn Bay at Pirita and identifies its surroundings as an important settlement and movement area since prehistory, supported by archaeological sites including Iru Linnapära. This supports a river-valley travel landscape, not an exact bridge at the later convent site.
2. Archaeological research at Kloostri 16 on the right bank found early traces of movement from the 14th-16th centuries, but no building predating the later riverside farmstead on the excavated 900 m² plot. Natural layers included river sand, reed impressions, clay and river mud. This favors an open wet meadow/reed-bank treatment and argues against inventing a dense 1343 village at the future convent plot.
3. Pirita Convent's own history dates the founding idea to about 1400, advisers to 1407, construction to 1417 and consecration to 1436. No convent fabric belongs in a spring 1343 scene.
4. The project's livestock review records cattle, sheep, goats, pigs and horses as the principal domestic mammals in studied medieval and early modern Estonian assemblages, while warning that remains do not prove a particular live-animal density or plot. Horses therefore appear as two restrained working/tethered silhouettes and sheep as one small pasture group.
5. The project's archaeobotanical review supports cultivated grains and medieval plant remains only at regional scale. Field, fallow, hay and meadow shares remain bounded composition choices rather than site measurements.

## Map translation

- A broad two-depth river ribbon with muddy/reed banks crosses the map south-to-north.
- A timber deck and railings form the only dry crossing and preserve water blocking everywhere else.
- The west approach leads conceptually back to Reval and Viru Gate; the east road continues toward Iru and Harju.
- One croft west of the river and one loose farmstead east of it keep built density low.
- Three field strips and a fallow margin sit west of the river; hay meadow and the future convent meadow remain open on the east bank.
- Sheep occupy a fenced pasture. One horse is tethered by the east farm and another marks pack traffic near the bridge. No urban stable row, merchant frontage, stone church or monastery silhouette is used.
