# Ellen Luik (Midwife and Keeper of Old Songs)

**Confidence label:** `plausible composite`  
**Role:** Folklore perspective and keeper of community memory  
**Campaign presence:** After the vertical slice

## Motivations and core

- **Want:** Preserve people and memory rather than restore an imagined, uniform pagan state.
- **Fear:** Young radicals will turn fragmentary traditions into a justification for revenge.
- **Contradiction:** She encourages ambiguous supernatural belief because it gives frightened people courage, even when she cannot prove that belief is literally true.
- **Secret or withheld fact:** Ellen has deliberately changed parts of old songs over time, hiding names and places that could expose families who still keep household rites.

## Relationships

- **Kalev:** Recognizes that his maker's mark has become a public story as much as a craft mark, and challenges him to decide what that story protects.
- **Aita:** Shares remedies and household knowledge with her, though they disagree about when secrecy protects a community and when it isolates one.
- **Mart:** Understands his hunger for a heroic past but refuses to give him a pure ancestral cause.
- **Kaja:** Respects her restraint and fears that strategy will eventually make her treat people as expendable.

## Voice

Patient, precise, and unsentimental. Ellen distinguishes what she witnessed, what she heard, and what she merely hopes is true. Her images come from songs, births, seasons, and remembered places rather than a codified magical vocabulary.

## Possible outcomes

- Preserves a truthful record of the district through the uprising.
- Turns Hingepuu into a humane ritual of reflection without confirming supernatural power.
- Withdraws from Kalev after he weaponizes community belief.
- Leaves Reval with endangered families and carries their songs elsewhere.
