# Kalev Family and Household Canon

**Status:** active canon  
**Task:** P0-012  
**Confidence label:** `invented` unless otherwise noted  
**Sources:** [`README.md`](../../README.md) Main cast and Scope boundaries; [`docs/CANON.md`](../CANON.md)

This document resolves Kalev's immediate family and household for the first campaign. It supersedes any active-document ambiguity about Kalev's parents, siblings, partner, children, home, and relationship to Mart. Legacy or archived material may contain alternate relatives or later plotlines, but those are reference only unless reconciled here and in `docs/CANON.md`.

## Canon summary

- **Parents:** Kalev and Aita's parents are dead before the playable campaign begins. They are backstory only, not living NPCs in the first campaign. Their names and exact deaths are intentionally unresolved until a later character brief needs them.
- **Siblings:** Aita is Kalev's only named sibling and his older sister. No other sibling is active canon for the first campaign.
- **Partner:** Kalev has no living spouse, romantic partner, or betrothed during the first campaign.
- **Children:** Kalev has no children.
- **Home:** Kalev lives and works at his lower-town smithy. The forge is his home, workbench, social hub, and mission source; the playable household spaces are the smithy interior and its small courtyard.
- **Mart:** Mart is Kalev's 16-year-old apprentice, not his son, brother, nephew, or blood relative. Kalev is responsible for Mart as master of the forge and as a protective adult, which creates a family-like emotional stake without making Mart kin.

## Relationships

### Aita

Aita is Kalev's older sister, an alewife and healer with a separate household and work life. Their shared family history gives her authority to challenge Kalev's excuses, but she is not subordinate to his forge household.

### Mart

Mart lives or lodges under the practical authority of the smithy as an apprentice when the story requires it, but his bond to Kalev is craft, duty, and chosen loyalty rather than blood. Kalev wants to protect him; Mart resents what he sees as Kalev's caution and secretly passes rejected spearheads bearing Kalev's maker mark to the Black Cloaks.

### Parents

Kalev and Aita inherited memory, tools, habits, and household rites from their parents, but the parents do not appear as living characters. Avoid adding dialogue that implies either parent is alive in 1343 unless this document and `docs/CANON.md` are updated first.

### Partner and children

Kalev's first-campaign emotional stakes are Mart, Aita, the forge, the lower-town community, and his public name. Do not introduce a wife, husband, lover, betrothed, son, or daughter for Kalev in active first-campaign documents without a new canon decision.

## Implementation notes

- Use "master" or "smith" for Kalev's role toward Mart, not "father" except as metaphorical dialogue clearly marked as someone else's interpretation.
- The forge may be called Kalev's home, shop, smithy, workshop, or household. These refer to the same lower-town hub unless a later location document splits rooms more precisely.
- If a scene needs private family pressure, use Aita or Mart rather than inventing parents, a partner, or children.
