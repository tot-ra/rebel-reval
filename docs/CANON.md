# Canon: Reval Rebel

This document establishes the historical and narrative baseline for *Reval Rebel*. It organizes facts, characters, and terminology, clarifying what is historically attested, what is plausible within the historical context, what is drawn from folklore, and what is invented for the game's narrative.

## Confidence Labels

Every entry in this document uses one of the following confidence labels to guide implementation:
- **`attested`**: A verifiable historical fact, person, or term present in primary sources or established historiography.
- **`plausible composite`**: A fictional element built strictly from historically accurate archetypes, structures, or plausible events.
- **`folklore`**: An element drawn from period-appropriate or regionally appropriate myth, belief, or oral tradition, presented subjectively within the game.
- **`invented`**: Entirely fictional content created to serve the game's specific narrative, dramatic, or gameplay needs, restricted by the boundaries of the established history.

## Timeline (April–May 1343)

The playable narrative focuses on the days immediately preceding and during the St. George's Night Uprising.

* **Pre-April 23, 1343 (The Simmering City)** - **`invented`** (Narrative framing)
  * The game's five day/night cycles take place in the immediate build-up to the historical uprising. Tensions are high; factions are maneuvering. 
* **April 23, 1343 (St. George's Night)** - **`attested`** (Source: Chronicles of Balthasar Russow / Livonian Chronicle of Hermann de Wartberge)
  * A signal fire on a hilltop in Harju County triggers the uprising. Coordinated attacks begin on German nobles, merchants, and clergy. 
  * The "Four Kings" (Neli kuningat) are elected by the rebels.
* **Late April 1343** - **`attested`** (Source: Historical accounts of the siege)
  * Rebel forces from Harju County lay siege to Reval (Tallinn), demanding surrender from the Danish-held city.
* **May 11, 1343 (Battle of Kanavere Bog)** - **`attested`** (Source: Livonian Order records)
  * A minor victory for the Estonian rebels against the forces of the Livonian Order.
* **May 14, 1343 (Battle of Sõjamäe)** - **`attested`** (Source: Livonian Chronicle of Hermann de Wartberge; later chronicle tradition)
  * The decisive defeat of the main rebel army by Master Burchard von Dreileben’s Livonian Order forces near Lake Ülemiste. The mainland rebellion is largely crushed.

## Terminology

* **Reval** - **`attested`** 
  * The historical name for Tallinn, used by the Danish and German ruling classes and the Hanseatic League.
* **Toompea (Domberg)** - **`attested`**
  * The upper town / castle hill of Reval, seat of the Danish viceroy.
* **Harju County (Harjumaa)** - **`attested`**
  * The region surrounding Reval where the uprising ignited.
* **Livonian Order** - **`attested`**
  * An autonomous branch of the Teutonic Knights, the de facto military power in the region, operating from strongholds outside Reval.
* **St. George's Night Uprising (Jüriöö ülestõus)** - **`attested`**
  * The modern Estonian name for the 1343 rebellion.
* **The "Four Kings"** - **`attested`**
  * Four Estonian leaders chosen by the rebels to lead the siege of Reval; later treacherously killed by the Livonian Order at Paide.
* **Hingepuu** - **`invented`** / **`folklore`**
  * The reflection entity in the game, inspired by Baltic animist concepts of the "soul tree" or sacred groves, but formalized as a game mechanic to confront Kalev with his choices.

## Names & Pronunciation

*Pronunciation guides use approximate English phonetics.*

### Main Cast

* **Kalev** (The Smith) - **`invented`**
  * *Pronunciation:* KAH-lev
  * *Notes:* Lower-town smith, the player character. Kalev lives and works at his lower-town smithy; see [Kalev Family and Household Canon](./CHARACTERS/kalev-family.md) and [Kalev Character Brief](./CHARACTERS/kalev.md).
* **Mart** (The Apprentice) - **`invented`**
  * *Pronunciation:* MAHRT
  * *Notes:* Kalev's 16-year-old apprentice, not his blood relative. See [Mart Character Brief](./CHARACTERS/mart.md).
* **Aita** (Alewife & Healer) - **`invented`**
  * *Pronunciation:* EYE-tah
  * *Notes:* Kalev's older sister and only named sibling in active canon. See [Aita Character Brief](./CHARACTERS/aita.md).
* **Kaja** (Bilingual Courier) - **`invented`**
  * *Pronunciation:* KAH-yah
  * *Notes:* Operates between the rural rebels and the city. See [Kaja Character Brief](./CHARACTERS/kaja.md).
* **Captain Henning** (Commander of Viru Watch) - **`plausible composite`**
  * *Pronunciation:* HEN-ning
  * *Notes:* Fictional composite representing the local city watch authority. See [Henning Character Brief](./CHARACTERS/henning.md).
* **Jürgen Witte** (Hanseatic Merchant) - **`plausible composite`**
  * *Pronunciation:* YUR-gen VIT-teh
  * *Notes:* Profiteering merchant and political opportunist. See [Jürgen Character Brief](./CHARACTERS/jurgen.md).
  * *Notes:* Replaces placeholder "Jürgen von League". Amber merchant representing Hanseatic economic interests.
* **Ellen Luik** (Midwife) - **`plausible composite`**
  * *Pronunciation:* EL-len LOO-ik
  * *Notes:* Baptized midwife and keeper of old songs, bridging Christian practice and older traditions. See [Ellen Character Brief](./CHARACTERS/ellen.md).

### Historical Figures (Mentioned/Background)

* **Valdemar IV Atterdag** - **`attested`**
  * *Pronunciation:* VAL-deh-mar (At-ter-dag)
  * *Notes:* King of Denmark in 1343; distant ruler of the Duchy of Estonia.
* **Burchard von Dreileben** - **`attested`**
  * *Pronunciation:* BOOR-khart fon DRY-lay-ben
  * *Notes:* Master of the Livonian Order who defeated the rebels at Sõjamäe.

### Key Locations & Events (Pronunciation)

* **Jüriöö** - YOO-ree-er (ö is like the 'ur' in 'fur')
* **Sõjamäe** - SER-yah-may (õ is a mid-back unrounded vowel, similar to 'uh' or Russian 'ы'; ä is like 'a' in 'cat')
* **Kanavere** - KAH-nah-veh-reh
* **Harjumaa** - HAR-yoo-mah
