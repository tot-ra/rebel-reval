# Berthold Schwarz

**Dates:** fl. 14th century

A legendary German alchemist and Franciscan monk, often credited with the invention or independent discovery of gunpowder in Europe. While his historical existence is debated, with some scholars considering him a symbolic figure, he is said to have been active in the 14th century. His work, whether real or allegorical, represents the arrival of gunpowder technology in Europe, a development that would fundamentally change warfare and society.
